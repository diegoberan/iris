AMD compute, demonstrably

Your GPU first. AMD cloud when you need it.

One OpenAI-compatible route lets the Brain use the closest capable hardware, then advance when a local body goes offline.

Local

RX 9060 XT

Gemma runs beside Dot's F5-TTS voice on the user's own Radeon — private, available, and first in line.

AMD Cloud

MI300 · vLLM

The current cloud runtime hosts Gemma on an AMD MI300 pod when local capacity is unavailable or scale matters.

Fireworks AI

A tested resilience route.

The Fireworks API is wired as the last-resort route. Stop a local body and the next turn advances without losing the Brain.

Validated across the stack

Íris was also tested on the hackathon's Radeon PRO W7900 pod, serving Gemma Q4 through llama.cpp. The project therefore proves the same routing idea across local Radeon, AMD cloud via vLLM, workstation-class AMD hardware, and the Fireworks AI API.

The protocol behind the product

Why Íris exists.

01

Persistent, not session-based

Your AI doesn't reset when you close a tab. It's a brain, always on.

02

Bodies, not clients

Every device is an extension of the same agent — eyes, ears, a voice, not a login screen.

03

Capabilities, not apps

Devices announce what they can do. The brain decides where each action runs.

04

Compute goes where it's cheapest

Your GPU first, the cloud when needed — routed live, any model.

05

Your own brain

Its memory, its data, its infrastructure. Not ours.

06

Always yours

No platform lock-in, no data hostaging, and your data is never used to train foreign models. You choose the rules.

* Disclaimer: With great power comes great responsibility. You can configure the brain however you want and grant any permissions you see fit, but security, reliability, and correctness remain under your responsibility. Note: LLMs can hallucinate.
